Tomato Early Blight


Prediction:

Early blight creates brown lesions with concentric rings on older leaves. It weakens the plant and spreads upward from the bottom. Prevention:
Improve air circulation, mulch the soil, and apply fungicides when symptoms appear.

Tomato Late Blight


Prediction:

Late blight produces large, water‑soaked lesions that turn brown or black. It is highly destructive and can kill the plant quickly. Prevention:
Remove infected plants immediately, avoid wet foliage, and use preventive fungicides in wet seasons.

Tomato Leaf Mold


Prediction:

Leaf mold appears as yellow spots on top of leaves and olive‑green mold underneath. It thrives in high humidity. Prevention:

Tomato Spider Mites (Two‑spotted Spider Mite)


Prediction:

Infestation causes tiny yellow speckles and fine webbing. Leaves may dry out as mites feed on sap. Prevention:

Target spot forms brown lesions with concentric rings. Severe infections reduce fruit quality. Prevention:

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV)


Prediction:

This virus causes leaf curling, yellowing, and stunted growth. Infected plants produce fewer fruits. Prevention:
Control whiteflies (the main vector), remove infected plants, and use resistant varieties.
